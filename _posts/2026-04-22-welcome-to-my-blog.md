I've spent my career as a management consultant working across the mining and energy sectors. Like most people in this industry, I've been watching the development of AI closely over the past few years. It's moving quickly, and it's going to fundamentally change how companies in this space operate.


That's both a risk and an opportunity. The companies that find ways to work effectively with AI stand to make real gains across their operations and outcomes. Those that don't will likely find themselves behind.


I've become particularly interested in AI agents, and I believe they'll be one of the more significant developments as this technology matures. The ability to automate not just tasks but entire workflows, with something that can reason and act, has real implications for how mining and energy companies are structured and how they make decisions.


With that in mind, I've started building a series of agents designed around workflows relevant to these industries. The intent is proof of concept, to identify areas where agentic AI could add genuine value. If anything like this were to be rolled out at an operator, there would be considerably more work required around governance, coordination, change management, the intricacies of existing workflows, and how these tools interact with the people using them.


There's no shortage of conversation about AI on LinkedIn. This is my attempt to move from talking about it to actually building it, and to share what I learn in the process.
