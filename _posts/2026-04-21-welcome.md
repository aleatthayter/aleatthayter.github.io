I am a Manager at Monitor Deloitte, where I have spent the past five years working with mining and energy companies on AI and digital strategy. That work covers a range of things: helping operators define where AI fits in their business, building the governance frameworks that sit around it, designing the roadmaps that take them from current state to something more capable, and working through what these changes mean for the people and processes involved.

That background gives me a reasonably clear view of where AI is heading in these industries and what it will take to get there. It also means I have spent a lot more time advising on AI than actually building any of it, and at some point that started to feel like a gap worth closing.

Agents are where I think the most interesting things are happening right now. The ability to automate not just a single task but an entire workflow, with something that can reason and make decisions along the way, has real implications for how mining and energy companies operate. The agents that matter are not the ones that generate a summary or answer a question. They are the ones that sit inside a real operational process, take inputs from existing systems, and produce outputs that people can act on.

With that in mind, I have started building a series of agents designed around workflows I know from the field. The intent is proof of concept. I am not trying to build production software. I am trying to understand what is genuinely achievable, where the real complexity sits, and what I did not know about building these things that I thought I did from the advisory side. There is a meaningful difference between designing an AI strategy and actually shipping something, and I think that difference matters.

If anything like this were to be deployed at an operator, there would be considerably more work required around governance, change management, integration with existing systems, and how these tools interact with the people using them. That work is real and it is not trivial. But it starts with knowing what is possible.

There is no shortage of conversation about AI on LinkedIn. This is my attempt to move from talking about it to actually building it, and to share what I learn along the way.

Andrew
